Facts – Concent of Saunt Edhar

Neal Stephenson: Anathem. New York: William Morrow, 2008. ISBN 978-0-06-147410-1. Edition: Harper-Collins eBooks.

General Area

Terrain

  • terminus of mountain range stretching hundreds of miles to northeast[1], w/ glaciers and slowpacked peaks
  • mountains are headwater of river[2]
  • below mountain terminus, a prairie[3]
  • in foothills, an abandoned quarry (where people practice shooting)[4], distance to city unspecified

River (flows north to south)

  • more upstream (well above Mynster level and due north of it): concent pool (open cistern)[5]
  • emptying into aqueduct runs due south, bypassing cataract, bridge [which?], bend[6]
  • first tunnel
  • then aqueduct on stilts of 0.5 mi length over broken terrain
  • then pipe under local city’s “burger” neighborhood[7]
  • ends in pond in front of Day Gate
  • upstream of concent: cataracts (noisy)
  • river curves across prairie[8]
  • dodges around rocky bluffs of concent[9] / concent nestled in crook of river[10]
  • 1.5 miles of river inside concent walls[11]
  • navigable at least by punts
  • seriously navigable some distance downstream of concent[12]
  • downstream: river becomes navigable (before Ancient Steel Bridge)
  • more downstream: tributary joins river from the west[13]

City (north to south)

  • plaza north of Day Gate is central square[14]
  • north central quare plaza: burger neighborhood
  • between Day and Decade gate: commercial district
  • larger road running (invisible to Decade Gate due to curve[15]) roughly parallel to river
  • west of Decade Gate, across larger road: burger neighborhood hill
  • burger neighborhood hill [also logically] ends before “ancient steel bridge”
  • south of Decade Gate, workshops eventually give way to warehouses (and river becomes navigable)[16]
  • more south: Ancient Steel Bridge
  • crosses river tributary[17], winding through floodplain
  • steel bridge 1 mi away from river[18]
  • even more south: “fauxburbs”[19]
  • “trailers without wheels”[20]
  • at unspecified location: an “aerodrome”[21]
